The Sun

First thing to know about the sun is that it's actually a star! It looks so big because we are so much closer to it than other stars, so much so that we can see it during the day. But don't look directly at it! Staring at the sun will hurt your eyes! So what exactly is a star? A star is a hot, glowing ball of gas.

But how close are we to the Sun? The answer is much closer than other stars, but still pretty far away. (and a good thing too!) If we were too close, it would be way too hot to live on Earth. The Sun is about 150 million kilometers or 93 million miles away from Earth. That's 400 times farther than the distance between Earth and the Moon! The Sun's surface is very hot, and its atmosphere is even hotter. And the Sun's core is the hottest part of all, about 15 million degrees Celsius and 27 million degrees Fahrenheit!

The Sun is about 100 times wider than Earth, but that's a pretty normal size for stars! Astronomers have found some stars that are 100 times bigger than the Sun and others that are 10 times smaller.The Sun's gravity holds our entire solar system together. Our solar system is even named after the Sun! Thats why it's called the “solar system!” Sol is the latin word for Sun.The gravitational pull of the Sun is massive! It's what keeps everything in the solar system revolving around it. The heat that comes from the Sun makes the Earth warm enough to live on. Without light from the Sun, there would be no plants or animals, the sunlight helps grow the plants, sustain life, we and animals eat the plants and we eat the animals. So, it would be impossible to have life on the planet without the Sun!

Something to know about stars in general is that they have a cycle of life! The Sun is right smack in the middle of its lifecycle. Don't worry though, we will have our Sun for a very long time. Right now, our Sun is in a stage called yellow dwarf. It is about 4.5 billion years old. In another 5 billion years the Sun will become a big, cooler temperature star called a red giant. A few billion years after that, it will become a small white dwarf star. It will shrink to around the same size as Earth, except the sun would weigh 20,000 more!

Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet in the solar system. It's just a little bigger than the Moon! Mercury itself, though, doesn't have its own moons. It is the closest planet to the Sun, but it's actually not the hottest. That humble title would go to Venus. Along with Venus, Earth, and Mars, Mercury is one of the rocky planets. It has a solid surface that is covered with craters. Instead of an atmosphere, Mercury possesses a thin exosphere made up of atoms blasted off the surface by the solar wind and striking meteoroids.Mercury's exosphere is composed mostly of oxygen, sodium, hydrogen, helium, and potassium. Mercury is a terrestrial planet. Mercury's surface can be as hot as 800 degrees F during the daytime and as cold as -300 degrees F during the nighttime.

This small planet spins around slowly compared to Earth, so one day lasts a long time. It takes 59 Earth days to make one day (or one full rotation) on Mercury. However, a year on Mercury goes by extremely fast. Because it's the closest planet to the Sun, it doesn't take very long to go all the way around. It completes one revolution around the Sun in just 88 Earth days. If you lived on Mercury, you'd have a birthday every three months! A day on Mercury is very different from a day here on Earth. For us, the Sun rises and sets every day. Mercury spins too slow for it to be the same on that planet! It takes much longer for the sun to rise and set there. Mercury only has one sunrise every 180 Earth days. That means it takes 180 of our days to have ONE day on Mercury!

What does terrestrial mean?

Terrestrial refers to planets that are similar to earth in some ways like density or what it's made of (composition!)

Venus

Venus is the hottest planet. It has a thick atmosphere full of the greenhouse gas carbon dioxide and clouds made of sulfuric acid. The gas traps heat and keeps Venus super duper warm. In fact, it's so hot on Venus, metals like lead would be puddles of melted liquid. Its a very unique planet, it spins the opposite direction to earth and rotates so much slower!

How slow you may ask? It takes roughly 243 days on Earth for Venus to spin just once! Not only that but it takes 225 Earth days for Venus to go around the Sun, making a day on Venus longer than a year. Isn't that amazing?

Venus has mountains and volcanoes just like Earth! But similar to Mercury, it's smaller than Earth and has no moons.

Earth

Earth is the planet we all live on! Despite what you may have heard about "boys going to Jupiter." Its surface is solid and liquid with mountains, volcanoes, plains, forests and ocean. The ocean is truly what makes Earth unique. The ocean covers 70% of the entire planet's surface!

Earth has an atmosphere, not just an exosphere like Mercury. It is made up of gasses and has six layers! The troposphere, the stratosphere, the mesosphere, the thermosphere, the ionosphere, and the exosphere. It keeps the planet warm and gives us oxygen to breathe. If it wasn't for the atmosphere, we would probably live on a planet with many more crators, because it protects us from meteoroids that fly through space. Life on Earth is sustained by the atmosphere and the heat from the Sun, we have water to drink which isn't present on other planets. Aren't we lucky?

Our days here are about 24 hours as I'm sure you know, and our years are considered to be 365.25 days! What is the .25 you may ask? Well, it refers to the occasional leap year/leap day.

Mars

Mars is a cold desert world. The great inspiration of science fiction. The average temperature on Mars is minus 85 degrees Fahrenheit or minus 65 degrees Celsius. These are super-duper below the freezing point. It is half the size of Earth. Mars is sometimes called the Red Planet. It's red because of rusty iron in the ground.


Like Earth, Mars has seasons, polar ice caps, volcanoes, canyons, and weather. It's a terrestrial planet just like Mercury, Venus and Earth. It has a very thin atmosphere made mostly of carbon dioxide, nitrogen, and argon. Mars' atmosphere has clouds and wind just like Earth. Sometimes the wind blows the red dust into a dust storm. Spiraling dust storms called dust devils can look a lot like tornados. Mars' large storms sometimes cover the entire planet. There is barely any oxygen on Mars, so with the other elements listed, it would be impossible for us to breathe on Mars.


There are signs of ancient floods on Mars, but now water mostly exists in icy dirt and thin clouds. On some small parts of Mars, scientists found evience for salty water in the ground.


Scientists want to know if Mars may have had living things in the past. They also want to know if Mars could support life now or in the future.

A day on Mars is just a little over 24 hours. 24.6 to be exact. But it takes much longer for Mars to make its way around the Sun, so a year on Mars is 687 Earth days. Thats nearly twice as long!

Mars not only has a year thats twice as long as ours, but has twice as many moons! Mars' two moons have specific names, Phobos and Deimos.

Jupiter

Jupiter is huge! It's the biggest planet in the Solar System and o' so' very stormy. Its similar in many ways to a star in what it's made of, but never got big enough to become one! So a planet it remains. It is mostly made of hydrogen and helium and has a super duper thick atmosphere. It is covered in swirling multicolored stripes of gas clouds and has bigger storms that easily imagined like the one scientists call the Great Red Spot! The Great Red Spot has been around for hundreds of years. Can you imagine a storm that has been a storm the entire time youve been alive, your parents been alive, your gradparents and generations before?


Scientists say that its still unclear if deep inside, Jupiter has a solid central core or if its a thick, extremely hot, dense soup. Jupiter also has rings, but they're too faint to see very well. Saturns rings are much thicker.

A day on Jupiter is really short! Just ten hours long! But a year? Well, one Jupiter year is 11.8 Earth years long.


Jupiter had the championship belt for the most moons for most of history. It has 95 moons. More mass = more gravity to pull in moons! Many more moons may exist but they are not confirmed! But a historic moon-rivalry with Saturn nontheless.

Saturn

The rings we see are made of groups of tiny ringlets that surround Saturn. They're made of chunks of ice and rock. Like Jupiter, Saturn is mostly a ball of hydrogen and helium.

Saturn has a thick atmosphere like Jupiter. It also has a lovely set of seven main rings with spaces between them. These rings are what Saturn is most known for. One day on Saturn goes by in just 10.7 hours. One year on Saturn is the same as 29 Earth years. As of March 2025, Saturn has also taken the championship belt from Jupiter for the most "confirmed" moons with 274. Saturn's gravity has this kind of pull as a combination of factors between its distance from the Sun and its own size!(Even though its not as big as Jupiter) Historically, its the furthest planet from the sun spotted with just a person's eyes. No telescope! Though a telescope would help you very much.

Uranus

Uranus is made of water, methane, and ammonia fluids above a smaller solid rock center. It has lots of water, but unlike Earth it is uninhabitable and doesn't also have a solid surface! Its atmosphere is made of hydrogen and helium like Jupiter and Saturn, but it also has methane. Methane is actually what gives Uranus that beautiful blue color!. Uranus also has faint rings. The inner rings are narrow and dark. The outer rings are brightly colored and easier to see. Uranus, like Venus also has its own funky rotation. But while Venus rotates in the opposite direction to the other planets, Uranus uniquely rotates on its side!

Uranus is surrounded by a set of 13 rings. (Theyre just a bit faint!) Uranus is an ice giant (instead of a gas giant like Jupiter!). It is mostly made of flowing icy materials above a solid core. One day on Uranus lasts a little over 17 hours (17 hours and 14 minutes, to be exact). It takes 84 Earth years to get ONE year on Uranus. That's a lifetime!To top off our facts, it has 28 known moons!

Neptune

Neptune is very dark, very cold, and verrrry windy. It's the last of the planets in our solar system, which means its the furthest planet from the Sun! Neptune is super similar to Uranus. It's made of a thick fog of water, ammonia, and methane over an Earth-sized solid center. Its atmosphere is made of hydrogen, helium, and methane. The methane gives Neptune the same blue color as Uranus. Neptune has six rings, but they're much harder to see than the other planets rings

One day on Neptune is 16 hours long! One year on Neptune is 165 Earth years long! That's because of how amazingly far from the Sun Neptune is! That's the longest year we've ever heard of!

Neptune has 16 moons, and it's so far from the sun that only Uranus is its neighbor! A lonely planet indeed.
