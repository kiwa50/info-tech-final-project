About the Authors

By Daniela Franceschetti, Joanne Guerra, Katie Iwagami, and Sam Schlinkert

This website was created by Daniela Franceschetti, Joanne Guerra, Katie Iwagami, and Sam Schlinkert for our final group website project in Pratt Institute's School of Information. Together, we created an educational space website about NASA history, satellites, the solar system, women in space, and STEM learning.

Daniela Franceschetti

Daniela Franceschetti is a student in Pratt Institute's School of Information. Her interests include storytelling, creativity, and collaborative world-building through live action role-playing. For this project, she contributed to the development of the website's content and overall educational focus.

Joanne Guerra

Joanne Guerra is a student in Pratt Institute's School of Information. In addition to her graduate studies, she works in real estate and is interested in living on the moon. For this project, she contributed to the website's research, writing, and organization.

Katie Iwagami

Katie Iwagami is a first year student in the MSLIS program at Pratt Institute's School of Information. This is her first group website project and second assignment using GitHub. She worked on the "Women in Space" page, wrote the "Welcome" text of the homepage, chose the background image of the webpages, and worked on applying the favicon to the website design with Joanne.

Sam Schlinkert

Sam Schlinkert is working toward his MLIS at Pratt Institute. He's written HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and Rust in the past. For this project, he wrote the code for the "Photo of the Day" section above, which queries a NASA API, as well as the "Suit Up!" and Satellites interactives.
