Welcome

Welcome to our website! If you're looking for information on space exploration you've come to the right place. We invite you to check out our webpages that have lots of cool facts about the solar system, astronauts, and the history of NASA. Have you ever wondered what experience you need to be a part of NASA, and what astronauts get to research while in space? Read about a few of the incredible women who have made significant contributions to astronomy research! Did you know Mercury can reach up to 800°F on its surface? See our page on solar system facts! Want to know more about NASA? The Moon Landing, and current NASA projects? Check out the page on NASA History! Imagine yourself as an astronaut (scroll down!), and see what your name looks like created by images of natural landmarks in our satellites page! We hope you enjoy learning about the ins and outs of space exploration, astronomy and STEM (science, technology, engineering, and mathematics) research, and that it inspires you to imagine a future for yourself in these exciting career pathways!

About the Authors

Katie Iwagami is a first year student in the MSLIS program at Pratt Institute's School of Information. This is her first group website project and second assignment using GitHub. She worked on the "Women in Space" page, wrote the "Welcome" text of the homepage, chose the background image of the webpages, and worked on applying the favicon to the website design with Joanne.

Sam Schlinkert is working toward his MLIS at Pratt Institute. He's written HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and Rust in the past. For this project, he wrote the code for the "Photo of the Day" section above, which queries a NASA API, as well as the "Suit Up!" and Satellites interactives.
